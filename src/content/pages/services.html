Lead Dust Hazards

Why Conduct Lead Dust Sampling Tests?

Lead dust is the most common pathway for lead poisoning, especially in children. Even invisible lead dust particles can cause serious health problems. Our lead dust sampling tests identify contaminated areas and ensure your property is safe.

Tenant Protection

Landlords must provide safe housing. Regular lead dust testing demonstrates due diligence and protects tenants, especially families with young children.

Health Protection

Lead dust exposure can cause developmental delays, learning disabilities, and behavioral problems in children. Testing identifies hazards before they cause harm.

EPA Compliance

Federal law requires lead dust testing in certain situations, including after renovation work and for rental properties with children under 6 years old.

Post-Renovation Safety

After any renovation or remodeling work in pre-1978 homes, dust testing confirms that lead dust levels are safe before the property is reoccupied.

Early Detection

Regular dust testing catches contamination early, before it becomes a major health hazard or expensive remediation project.

Property Transactions

Lead dust testing is often required during real estate transactions to ensure compliance with disclosure laws and protect all parties involved.
